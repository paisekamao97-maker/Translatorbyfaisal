About ZabānLink

Language is a
bridge, not a wall

ZabānLink was born from a simple frustration: two people wanting to talk, separated by language. We built the bridge.

creator

Meet Faisal

👨‍💻
Faisal
Developer · Karachi, Pakistan 🇵🇰

Faisal is a self-driven developer from Karachi with a passion for building tools that solve real-world problems. Growing up in Pakistan, he witnessed firsthand the communication barriers that arise daily between Urdu and Chinese speakers — especially as Pakistan-China ties deepened through trade, infrastructure, and cultural exchange.

"I built ZabānLink because I saw colleagues on CPEC projects struggling to communicate with Chinese engineers. There were expensive interpreters, long delays, and misunderstandings that cost time and money. I knew AI could do better — and make it free."

The name ZabānLink comes from 'Zabān' (زبان), meaning 'language' or 'tongue' in Urdu and Persian — a word understood across South Asia and beyond. Combined with 'Link', it captures exactly what we do: link languages and link people.

Privacy was non-negotiable. Your API key stays on your device. Your conversations never touch our servers. ZabānLink is built on the principle that translation should be free, fast, and private — forever.

the story

How ZabānLink
was built

💡
The Idea
Faisal watches a Urdu-speaking manager and a Chinese engineer try to communicate through a translation app that kept getting Nastaliq script wrong — outputting Latin transliteration instead of proper اردو.
🔬
Research
Deep-dive into Whisper large-v3, Llama 3.3 70B, and Groq's inference API. The breakthrough: using strict JSON prompting to guarantee Nastaliq script in the 'ur' field and CJK characters in the 'zh' field.
🛠️
Building
Pure HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — no framework bloat. Mobile-first design. 4 translations per phrase (Urdu, Romanized, Chinese, English). Live edit panel with 650ms debounce translation.
🌉
ZabānLink
Launched as a free, browser-based PWA. No app store, no subscription, no credit card. Just speak and be understood — in اردو, 中文, and English.
